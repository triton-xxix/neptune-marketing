The Neptune Approach

Traditional email reactivation campaigns had failed. Open rates were sub-5%, and the few responses were spam complaints. We took a different approach:

1. AI-Powered Segmentation

We didn't blast everyone with the same message. Our AI analyzed each lead's original source, engagement history, and inferred intent to create micro-segments:

  • Quote Requesters: Had asked for pricing but never bought
  • Webinar Attendees: Engaged with content but no sales conversation
  • Content Downloaders: High intent signals, low follow-up
  • Lost Deals: Had conversations that went cold

2. Conversational SMS, Not Broadcasts

Instead of "Hey, still interested?" mass texts, we deployed AI SMS agents that could hold real conversations:

  • Referenced their original inquiry specifically
  • Asked intelligent follow-up questions
  • Handled objections in real-time
  • Qualified interest before human handoff
"The difference was night and day. Our old email campaigns felt like shouting into the void. The SMS conversations felt like a sales rep actually following up‚Äîexcept it was happening 24/7 with every single lead simultaneously."

3. Performance-Based Structure

Because we only get paid on results, we had skin in the game. Our AI iterated daily based on response patterns:

  • Adjusted messaging based on what converted
  • Optimized send times per segment
  • A/B tested conversation flows continuously

What Made This Work

Three factors separated this from typical "lead reactivation" attempts:

1. Context Preservation

The AI knew each lead's history. When a webinar attendee responded, it referenced the specific webinar topic. When a quote requester replied, it acknowledged their original inquiry. This wasn't cold outreach‚Äîit was warm follow-up at scale.

2. Two-Way Conversations

Most SMS automation is one-way blasts. Our AI actually held conversations, answering questions, handling "not right now" objections, and booking meetings when interest was clear.

3. Aligned Incentives

Our performance-based model meant we only made money when revenue was generated. This drove constant optimization that wouldn't happen with a fixed monthly retainer.
